Mathematical Model for Crack Growth and Synthetic Data Generation

This model integrates fracture mechanics, fluid dynamics, and wave propagation to simulate crack growth, pressure changes, and ultrasonic signal responses.

1. Crack Growth Model (Extended Paris Law)

The crack growth follows an extended Paris’ Law, incorporating stress corrosion cracking (SCC) and temperature dependence:

da/dt = C_T * (σ * πa)^m * (1 + SCC_factor) * C_x

Explanation of Terms:

  • a: Crack length (meters)
  • t: Time (seconds)
  • C_T: Temperature-dependent growth coefficient, modeled as:
    C_T = C_0 * e^(-μ / RT)
  • σ: Applied stress (Pascals)
  • m: Paris law exponent, an empirical constant
  • SCC_factor: Growth due to SCC:
    SCC_factor = α(h3S / C_threshold)^β
  • C_x: Random variability factor (simulated using Gaussian noise)

2. Leakage Pressure Drop Model (Fluid Dynamics)

Once a crack forms, it allows fluid leakage, causing a pressure drop:

ΔP = (1/2) * ρ * v^2

where velocity is given by:

v = sqrt(2 * (P1 - P2) / ρ)

Flow Rate through the Crack:

Q = C_d * A * v

where A is the crack opening area:

A = A_ref * (a / a_0)

3. Ultrasonic Wave Response (FEM Model)

The response follows a finite element model:

FEM_Response = sin(2πfa)

with noise added:

Wave_Signal = sin(2πft) + 0.5 * Burst_Noise

4. Spectral Entropy Computation

To quantify signal complexity:

H = -∑ p_i log2(p_i)

5. Feature Extraction for ML Training

  • Amplitude (A):
    max(Wave_Signal)
  • Duration (T_d):
    max(0.5, min(5, a * 1000))
  • Energy (E):
    (1/2) * A^2 * T_d
  • Rise Time (T_r):
    T_d / 10
  • Peak Frequency (f_p):
    max(10, min(100, 50 + a * 1000))
  • RMS Voltage (V_rms):
    A / 10
  • Signal Attenuation (S_att):
    max(0.1, min(5, a * 100 * M * D))

6. Final Output: Hybrid Dataset

The dataset includes:

  • Crack growth history
  • Pressure variations
  • Ultrasonic signal responses
  • Extracted ML features

This dataset trains ML models for automated damage detection.
